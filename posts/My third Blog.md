In this stage, our group focused on developing wireframes to represent the structure of our application. Instead of only thinking about ideas or features, we started to look at how the system is actually organised into different pages and how users move through them.

The wireframes we created show different parts of the system, including the entry page, the room-related pages, and the chat interface. Each of these pages has a clear role. For example, the entry page is used to bring users into the system, the room pages allow users to enter a space, and the chat page is where interaction happens. Thinking about the system in this way helped us understand how the whole experience is structured.

One important decision we made during this stage was to keep the interface simple. Since our concept focuses on anonymity and low-pressure interaction, we did not want the interface to feel overwhelming. Most of the pages follow a clear layout with one main area and only a few supporting elements. This helps users focus on interaction instead of trying to understand a complicated interface.

For the chat interface, we designed it to support communication while reducing the visibility of identity. Messages are displayed in a simple format, without strong emphasis on usernames or profiles. This design shifts attention away from individuals and towards the interaction itself. At the same time, we still keep enough structure so that conversations are easy to follow.

The entry and room-related pages are also designed to be minimal. We limited the number of options available to users. This is because giving users too much control could reduce the sense of randomness. By keeping the process simple, users can enter the system more quickly and experience more spontaneous interaction.

Another key focus in this stage was understanding how these pages connect together. We started to think about the system as a flow instead of separate screens. A user first enters the platform, then moves into a room, and finally interacts through the chat. This step-by-step structure helped us design the system as a continuous experience.

Overall, this stage helped us move from abstract ideas to a clearer structure. Instead of adding more features, we focused on organising the system and making sure every part supports the main concept. In the next stage, we will begin implementing these wireframes using HTML and CSS and test how well they work in practice.
