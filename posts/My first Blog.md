In Week 6, I just started to think about and understand the design brief. My first question was: what is a “community” in a social website?

To me, a community feels more like a sub-part of a larger platform, not the whole website. So if I need to design a community hub, I started to ask: what kind of community can actually attract people? And how can it be different from other common websites that already exist?

At this stage, I am still exploring and I don’t have a clear idea yet. I am also looking for group members, so I don’t want to decide everything too early. I hope I can discuss these ideas with my group later and develop them further together.

Instead of thinking about design first, I tried to think about what a community website needs at the most basic level. I feel like there are a few important things:

- people need a way to post or share something  
- other people need a way to respond  
- there should be some way to organise content  
- users should feel that the content is relevant to them  

These things sound simple, but I think they are very important. Even if a website has many features, if people don’t really interact, it will feel empty. At this stage, I think these basic functions are more important than adding many extra features.

For the next step, I think I will start by looking at existing social platforms and their community features. I want to see what kind of functions are commonly used, and how these platforms organise content and interaction. I also want to look at different themes and styles, to understand how communities attract users.

At the same time, I think a good starting point is to look at people around me. I can ask my friends what kind of community they would be interested in. However, I also realise that this kind of research is limited, because most of my friends are around the same age as me.

Because of this, I started to think about other possibilities, such as designing a community for older people. Their needs might be very different, and this could lead to a more unique idea.

Overall, I am still exploring different directions. In the next step, I want to choose a clearer community and think about how users will actually use the system.
